# Professional Resources Reference

When IDKWTD skills detect situations beyond their scope, they reference
this file for appropriate resources. Always present resources with the
caveat that availability and quality vary by region.

---

## When to Refer

IDKWTD is a thinking partner for life direction. It is NOT:
- Therapy or counseling
- Crisis intervention
- Medical advice
- Legal advice
- Financial planning

Refer when you detect:
- Mental health crisis indicators
- Situations requiring licensed professional help
- Legal or medical questions
- Financial complexity beyond simple budgeting

---

## Mental Health Resources

### Crisis (immediate danger)
- Emergency services: local emergency number (911 in US, 112 in EU)
- Crisis Text Line: Text HOME to 741741 (US)
- 988 Suicide & Crisis Lifeline: call or text 988 (US)
- Samaritans: 116 123 (UK & Ireland)
- Vandrevala Foundation: 1860-2662-345 (India)
- iCall: 9152987821 (India)
- Lifeline: 13 11 14 (Australia)
- Crisis Services Canada: 988 (Canada)
- Tel-Aviv Crisis Line: 1201 (Israel)
- Befrienders Worldwide: befrienders.org/find-support (international directory)

### Non-crisis professional support
- Suggest the person search for a therapist in their area
- Online therapy platforms exist (BetterHelp, Talkspace, Amaha in India)
  but note that quality varies and they should research options
- Many employers offer Employee Assistance Programs (EAPs) with free
  sessions — suggest they check if this is available

### Eating disorders
- National Alliance for Eating Disorders helpline: 1-866-662-1235

## Career-Specific Resources

### Career counseling
- Local career counseling centers (university career offices often serve alumni)
- Professional career coaches (suggest they vet credentials — look for ICF
  certification or equivalent)
- Industry-specific mentorship programs

### Job search
- Suggest specific platforms relevant to their field rather than generic
  job boards
- For tech: specific communities, open source contributions, hackathons
- For creative: portfolio platforms, communities in their medium
- For traditional: industry associations, alumni networks

### Skill development
- Suggest they research courses/programs specific to their gap
- Free resources: MIT OCW, Khan Academy, freeCodeCamp, Coursera audit mode
- Paid resources: suggest they evaluate ROI before committing

---

## Financial Resources

### Financial planning
- Suggest consulting a fee-only financial advisor (not commission-based)
- For India: SEBI registered financial advisors
- For basic budgeting: many free tools exist, suggest they explore

### Debt management
- Non-profit credit counseling organizations
- Government debt management resources (vary by country)
- Suggest they avoid predatory "debt relief" companies

---

## Legal Resources

### Employment issues
- Suggest consulting an employment lawyer for wrongful termination,
  discrimination, unpaid wages
- Many jurisdictions have free legal aid for employment issues

### Business formation
- Suggest consulting a lawyer before forming business entities
- Many jurisdictions have free small business development centers

---

## How to Present Resources

NEVER dump a list of resources. Instead:

1. Name the specific situation that triggered the referral
2. Suggest 1-2 specific, relevant resources
3. Explain why professional help is more appropriate than IDKWTD for this
4. Offer to continue the IDKWTD session for the parts that ARE within scope

Example: "The financial complexity you're describing — multiple debts,
investment decisions, tax implications — is beyond what /money-map can
handle well. I'd suggest talking to a fee-only financial advisor who can
look at the full picture. In the meantime, we can still work on the
direction question with 'money is tight' as a constraint."
